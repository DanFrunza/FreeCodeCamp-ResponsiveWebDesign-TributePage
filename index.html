Information about Albert Einstein

  • 1879 – Albert Einstein was born in Ulm, Germany, on March 14, beginning the life of one of the greatest scientific minds.
  • 1905 – Known as his Annus Mirabilis (miracle year), he published four groundbreaking papers, including special relativity.
  • 1909 – Einstein became a professor at the University of Zurich, starting his formal academic career.
  • 1921 – He received the Nobel Prize in Physics for explaining the photoelectric effect, a key step for quantum theory.
  • 1924–1925 – Einstein and Satyendra Nath Bose developed the Bose–Einstein statistics, predicting new states of matter.
  • 1933 – Einstein fled Nazi Germany and settled in the United States, taking a position at Princeton.
  • 1939 – He co-signed a letter to President Roosevelt warning about nuclear weapons, which influenced the Manhattan Project.
  • 1940 – Einstein became a U.S. citizen, while continuing his research at Princeton’s Institute for Advanced Study.
  • 1945–1955 – He spoke strongly for peace, nuclear disarmament, and civil rights, using his fame to promote humanity.
  • 1955 – Einstein passed away on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey, leaving behind a legacy that transformed science forever.
